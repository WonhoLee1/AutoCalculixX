Discontinuous slope

Due to a change in slope the fluid depth changes. For small slope changes this effect is usually neglegible. For variations in such that changes significantly, the fluid depth will change according to

(208)

assuming the mass flow and specific energy remain constant (no loss). For instance, a significant decrease of slope will lead to a fluid depth increase if the flow is supercritical and a decrease if it is subcritical.

The following constants have to be specified on the line beneath the *FLUID SECTION,TYPE=DISCONTINUOUS SLOPE card:

  • width of the channel.
  • trapezoidal angle of the channel.
  • not used.
  • the slope at node 1 (first node in the topology of the element; for this element the slope must be given explicitly and is not calculated from the coordinates of the end nodes belonging to the element).
  • the slope at node 2 (thrid node in the topology of the element; for this element the slope must be given explicitly and is not calculated from the coordinates of the end nodes belonging to the element).
